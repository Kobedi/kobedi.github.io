Most Important Technical Decisions (ITDs)

What are they? Simply stated, they are the most important technical decisions that have a long and influential effect in the life cycle of a software product. They determine how the product will mature and the principle that will guide its metamorphosis.

ITDs Characteristics

The effects of an ITD are viral, they will simply propagate further in the product. They shape the personality of the software product, if good, it will differentiate the product from competition. ITDs usually stay with the product, throughout its life span. ITDs effects take time to show (positive or negative), hence a long-term vision is mandatory

ITDs Categories

  1. Architectural ITDs: This is the highest level of abstraction and is the point when we decide the proper architecture for the product — by analogy, this is the terrain under the house and the foundation of the house.
  2. Technological ITDs: Once we have the architecture in place, it's time to choose the proper databases, frameworks, libraries, services, and so on (technology stack), that give life to the architecture.
  3. Data Structure ITDs: Data structures are the building blocks (the bricks) of the product; they store our in-motion data and they are usually part of the solutions to certain problems that should be solved.
  4. Algorithm ITDs: Data structures and algorithms shouldn't make a good team, they should make a perfect team.

Cloud ITDs

  1. Cloud ITDs are really not different from those for software products running on the on-premise. The exception is that Cloud takes away some of the Categories.
  2. For example, Serverless Applications are event-driven and only constructs ‘servers’ when there is request. Implementing what is termed Ephemeral Functions.
  3. Serverless is an Architecture design pattern, thus the most crucial part of the pragmatic professional is missed. The cloud decouples the need for the professional the need to worry about how the architecture of the software products will be.
  4. In total honesty, this does not mean there is no server, just that the pragmatic professional is not involved in key architectural technical decision
  5. This approach, almost creates a thin line between a Technology and Architecture decision. For example AWS uses AWS Lambda and Azure Functions for Microsoft.
  6. Thus the Cloud can present a rather complex situation, where it might not be clear when are making ITD and under which category do they fall in.

Pragmatic Professional: Netflix, what happens when you hit Play

  2. Re-wrote and changed their architecture style from Monolithic to Microservices.
  3. Chose to provide their own CDN, called Open Connect ,instead of what is offered. A good technology decision with built-in smart video management.
  4. For their Transcoding at this scale, data structures to interact with video format and audio format at a very high velocity.
